The Shining (1980), US
A family heads to an isolated hotel for the winter, where a sinister presence influences the father into violence. At the same time, his psychic son sees horrifying forebodings from both the past and the future.
When Poland was a satellite state of Soviet Russia after WWII, Polish artists faced heavy censorship in their creative works. The one area of expression the government didn’t care about was film posters, and so Polish film poster variations became sites of experimentation and artistic expression. They often had elements of symbolism, abstraction and the surreal and usually didn’t accurately reflect the content of the film due to how unconstrained these poster artists were. For this reason they reflect the politics and historical context of post WWII Poland and make for some highly visually interesting and engaging posters.
